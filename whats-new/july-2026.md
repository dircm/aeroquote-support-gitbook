# July 2026

## Smarter aircraft setup

Adding and maintaining aircraft is faster: AeroQuote can guess the type from a registration, suggest typical costs, and save edits as you go.

### Type from registration

When you **Add Aircraft** and enter a **Registration**, AeroQuote looks up the type for you (live flight data first, then AI if needed).

* A progress panel shows what is happening while the lookup runs
* You can **Cancel lookup** at any time and pick the type yourself
* If a solid match is found, the type is applied and a short “Guessed …” banner appears
* If nothing reliable is found, the panel simply closes — no empty or unclear suggestions

See [Add Your First Aircraft](../getting-started/add-your-first-aircraft.md) and [Aircraft Details](../guides/aircraft/aircraft-details.md).

### Suggest costs for me

On an aircraft’s **Costings** step, click **Suggest costs for me**. After a short review you can apply suggested hourly rate and/or margin plus typical operating costs for your market, or skip and keep editing manually.

See [Costings](../guides/aircraft/costings.md).

### Auto-save on Details and Performance

On **Details** and **Flight Performance**, changes save automatically after you finish editing a field. There is no separate Save button on those steps — a short confirmation toast appears when a save succeeds.

### Images from a URL

On **Aircraft → Images**, you can still upload files, and you can also **add a photo from a public image URL** when the picture already lives on the web.

See [Aircraft Images](../guides/aircraft/aircraft-images.md).

***

## What you need to do

Nothing required. On your next aircraft add or costings edit:

1. Try entering a real registration in the Add Aircraft wizard and let type lookup finish (or cancel if you prefer)
2. On Costings, try **Suggest costs for me** and review before applying
3. Confirm Details / Performance edits still look right after leaving the page — they auto-save
