The Terms & Conditions manager lets you create and manage the T&Cs that customers must accept when viewing quotes online.

---

## Accessing Terms & Conditions

1. Go to **Settings** from the sidebar
2. Navigate to the Terms & Conditions section

---

## Why Use Terms & Conditions?

When you enable T&C requirements:

- Customers must accept your terms before accepting a quote online
- Creates a clear legal agreement
- Acceptance is timestamped and recorded
- Protects your business

---

## Creating Terms & Conditions

### Step 1: Add New T&Cs

1. Click **Add Terms & Conditions**
2. Enter a **Name** (e.g., "Standard Charter Terms")
3. Enter the full **Terms Text**
4. Click **Save**

### Step 2: Format Your Terms

The terms text supports:
- Plain text
- Line breaks for paragraphs
- Numbered lists for clauses

{% hint style="info" %}
Keep your T&Cs clear and readable. Customers are more likely to accept terms they can easily understand.
{% endhint %}

---

## Multiple Terms Documents

You can create multiple T&C documents for different situations:

| Example | Use Case |
|---------|----------|
| Standard Charter Terms | Normal passenger flights |
| Cargo Terms | Freight and cargo flights |
| Medical Transport Terms | Air ambulance services |
| International Terms | Cross-border flights |

---

## Requiring T&C Acceptance

To require acceptance on a quote:

1. Open the quote
2. In the quote settings, enable **Require T&C acceptance**
3. Select which T&C document to use
4. Save the quote

See also: [Require T&Cs to be accepted online](../quotes/require-t-and-cs-to-be-accepted-online.md)

---

## Viewing Acceptance Records

When a customer accepts T&Cs:

- Timestamp is recorded
- IP address may be logged
- Acceptance shows on the quote/booking record

---

## Editing Terms & Conditions

1. Find the T&C document in the list
2. Click **Edit**
3. Update the text
4. Click **Save**

{% hint style="warning" %}
Editing T&Cs doesn't affect already-accepted quotes. Existing acceptances remain tied to the version the customer agreed to.
{% endhint %}

---

## Deleting Terms & Conditions

1. Find the T&C document
2. Click **Delete**
3. Confirm deletion

{% hint style="warning" %}
You cannot delete T&Cs that are currently assigned to active quotes. Remove assignments first.
{% endhint %}

---

## Tips

- Have your T&Cs reviewed by a legal professional
- Keep language clear and customer-friendly
- Update regularly as regulations change
- Always use T&Cs for online quote acceptance
