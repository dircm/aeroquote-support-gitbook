# Understanding Analytics

Use Analytics to review how your operation is performing over time — booking volumes, status mix, top routes, on-time performance, and fleet hours.

## Prerequisites

* **Bookings** module enabled
* User role with manager-level booking access

***

## Step 1: Open Analytics

1. Click **Analytics** in the sidebar (under Bookings)
2. The page loads with the default **Last 30 days** period and **All Aircraft** selected

## Step 2: Filter the data

Use the controls in the page header:

| Control | Options |
|---|---|
| **Aircraft** | All Aircraft, or a single tail number |
| **Period** | 7 / 30 / 90 days, 6 months, 12 months |

Filters apply to every widget on the page simultaneously.

## Step 3: Read the overview stats

The top **Booking Stats** row summarises the selected period:

* Total bookings and confirmed count
* Revenue or hour totals (depending on your configuration)
* Average load or utilisation indicators

## Step 4: Review on-time performance

The **On-Time Performance** widget compares scheduled vs actual departure times for tracked flights. Use it to spot systemic delays on specific routes or aircraft.

## Step 5: Use the charts

| Widget | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Booking Status** | Pie or bar breakdown of booking statuses in the period |
| **Top Routes** | Most-flown city pairs by booking count |
| **Bookings Over Time** | Trend line of booking volume |
| **Fleet Utilisation** | Hours flown per aircraft |
| **Flight Performance** | Block-time and distance trends |

Hover chart segments for exact values where supported.

## Step 6: Drill into operations

Analytics is a reporting view — to act on a booking, open **Bookings** from the sidebar and search by code or route.
