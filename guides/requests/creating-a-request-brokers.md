# Creating a Request (Brokers)

Requests are the first step in your quoting workflow. As a broker, you estimate across **external operators' aircraft**, then generate a quote and send Requests for Quote (RFQs) to those operators in one step.

Looking for the operator edition? [Find it here](creating-a-request-operators.md).

***

## Before You Start

You'll need at least one aircraft linked to an external operator — see [Adding Aircraft with an External Operator](../request-for-quote/creating-an-aircraft-with-external-operator.md). These aircraft form your virtual fleet: AeroQuote estimates across them just like an operator estimates across their own.

***

## Opening a New Request

1. Click **Requests** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add new Request**

The Request page opens as a single workspace — details, flights, and aircraft estimates are all visible on one screen.

***

## Request Details

Capture the enquiry basics in the top-left section:

* **Status** — Active, Not Interested, or Call Back. Once a quote is generated the status shows **Quote Already Created** with a link.
* **Total Passengers** — used to flag aircraft that can't accommodate the group
* **Contact** — search for the customer or create one inline; they carry through as the main contact on the quote
* **Job Notes** — internal notes that carry through to the quote

***

## Flights

Add one or more **Charter** flight legs — departure, arrival, date and time. As you add legs, the estimates table recalculates live for every aircraft in your virtual fleet.

***

## Reviewing Estimates and Selecting Aircraft

Each aircraft row shows the estimated cost for the full itinerary, including ferry legs from its home base. Tick the **include** checkbox on each aircraft you want in the quote.

{% hint style="success" %}
Each included external aircraft has a **Create RFQ** toggle. Leave it on to automatically send that operator a Request for Quote when you generate; turn it off to include the aircraft without contacting the operator. The default per aircraft is set on the aircraft itself — see [Adding Aircraft with an External Operator](../request-for-quote/creating-an-aircraft-with-external-operator.md).
{% endhint %}

***

## Generate and Request Quotes

1. Click **Generate and Request Quote**
2. Review and edit the message that will go to each operator
3. Click **Send Emails**

AeroQuote creates the quote and sends an RFQ to each included external operator. Each RFQ's status is attached to the matching option on the quote, so you can see at a glance who has responded.

***

## What Happens Next

* Track operator responses on the [Requests for Quotes page](../request-for-quote/track-responses-to-a-rfq.md) or directly on the quote's option banners
* Manage and accept responses — see [Managing RFQ Responses](../request-for-quote/manage-rfq-responses.md)
* Send the finished quote to your customer from the quote's **Send** step
